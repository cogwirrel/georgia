About Me

Georgia’s compositions aim to utilise the unique tool music can provide to explore topics that may be divisive or difficult to approach using language. Her previous works have focussed on aspects of identity and lived experience. Born in Sydney, Australia, Georgia studied for her Bachelor’s degree in composition at the Royal College of Music, London, graduating with Honours. She then studied for her Master of Music in Composition at the Sydney Conservatorium of Music where she was supported by the Doris Burnett-Ford Scholarship and was part of the 2018-2019 Composing Women Program. She has had works premiered in venues such as the National Portrait Gallery, London, Dulwich Picture Gallery, St. Martin-in-the-Fields, National Sawdust, and the Sydney Opera House. In recent years, Georgia has worked with ensembles such as the Sydney Symphony Orchestra, Janáček Philharmonic Orchestra, Moorambilla Voices, Gondwana Junior, the Australian World Orchestra, Flautist Claire Chase, the Sydney Symphony Orchestra Fellows, Melbourne Symphony Orchestra, The Australian Ballet, Orchestra Victoria, and Sydney Chamber Opera. In 2021 Georgia will be working with the Sydney Symphony Orchestra Fellows as part of their ’50 Fanfares’ project and will have works premiered by the Sydney Conservatorium of Music Symphony Orchestra and by Western Australian Youth Orchestras as the recipient of the 2020 Carol Day Commission.

Seidler Diaries

Most Australians have seen many Seidler-designed buildings such as Australia Square and the Horizon Apartments, but not many know that as a young man he spent most of 1940-1941 in an internment camp in Canada.

This project explores his diary entries from the period, setting them to music which will be performed in one of his many buildings next year.

The diary entry we will be exploring is particularly emotive. It describes a moment of reflection on the anniversary of the beginning of the WWII, as Harry stands out in the cold and thinks about his loved ones far away.

The broader multi-composer project of which this is a part will showcase the music and voices of both eminent and less represented composers, engaging audiences the personal stories of an Australian cultural icon who endured a great deal of injustice and oppression, and went on to a career that enriched his adopted nation.

If you can donate to this project you will be personally giving this true story a platform to be heard and contributing to a changing compositional landscape in which new Australian music of composers with diverse backgrounds and identities can be showcased.

All donations to this project will be used to provide myself and Christopher with the financial means to realise this project. As this project has been competitively selected by Cultural Partnerships Australia to be supported as part of their 'MatchLab' initiative, all donations will be matched dollar for dollar!
